Gravity

  • High Concept: Try not to let realistic physics kill you. In space. Maybe with aliens. And asteroids.
  • Player Experience Goals: Accomplishment, Frustration, learning, planning
  • Genre: survival action
  • Game play: You've got an engine and some manuevering jets. And a computer, luckily - rotation doesn't stop on it's on in space, and spinning out sucks. Press the up arrow for thrust, and the left - right arrows for rotation. Try not to run into anything.
  • Prototype goal: What game mechanic is this prototype evaluating? Some of the P2 physics engine - apparently this is less of a 'space' realism engine and more of a friction/springs thing.
  • Features: Explosions Space Multiple planets with realistic gravity, rotation and orbits.
  • Setting/Premise: you launched your shuttle!
  • Story: none
  • Target Audience: everyone?
  • Play time: As long as you can stay alive. 1 - 2 min, I think.
  • Strategies: don't always hold the thrust/up arrow down. I might remap that to spacebar to get the point across better.
  • Prototype Assets: I made nearly everything from scratch. the music is from Musopen, the site with royalty free music. Bonus, it's the Air Force Band.
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Asteroids?
